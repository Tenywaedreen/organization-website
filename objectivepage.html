  • STEP for Life Uganda aims to enhance environmental sustainability through initiatives such as tree planting, opening drainage systems, and protecting international water resources. The organization focuses on community mobilization to raise awareness about the importance of environmental conservation.
  • The organization prioritizes food security by educating communities on modern farming techniques. This includes promoting practices like the use of manure, mulching, and crop rotation to improve agricultural productivity and ensure that families have access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food.
    • STEP for Life Uganda is committed to improving health outcomes by ensuring access to safe drinking water and promoting hygiene practices. The organization works with communities to identify their health needs and develop relevant projects aimed at enhancing maternal and child health while combating diseases such as HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, and waterborne illnesses.
  • The organization emphasizes the importance of research in informing its interventions. By collecting and disseminating factual information, STEP for Life Uganda seeks to influence policy changes that benefit marginalized communities.
    • STEP for Life Uganda focuses on building the capacities of its members across various districts to engage effectively with local authorities. This objective aims to empower members to monitor local governance performance and advocate for community needs.
